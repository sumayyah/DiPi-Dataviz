UC DAVIS CENTER FOR DESIGN
IN THE PUBLIC INTEREST (dipi)

Design Research
The UC Davis Center for Design In the Public Interest (dipi) offers design solutions to persistent problems affecting the public; researches collaborative, democratic design processes; and shares solutions and findings via Creative Commons licensing and scholarly publications. The newly launched Center was awarded seed funding through the UC Davis Office of Research Interdisciplinary Frontiers Program. Our multi-disciplinary team includes expertise in the humanities, arts, social sciences, engineering, and technology. It offers an experimental approach plus partnerships with faculty and students from diverse disciplines create unique opportunities to explore new methods and to work at the leading edge of a growing movement that connects human-centered design with social good.

TEAM

Susan Verba
Associate Professor, Department of Design, Cluster Leader/PI
Susan brings cross-disciplinary expertise in directing large collaborative project teams and developing innovative design methods, processes, and outcomes. Her academic research focuses on design for public benefit; she is particularly interested in addressing the needs of individuals outside the mainstream, including seniors, beginners, and persons with disabilities.
Yohei Kato
Master's student at UC Davis Community Development Graduate Group
Yohei Kato, a master's student at the UC Davis Community Development Graduate Group. His research interests include youth development and design that empowers community. He founded the competition in Dhaka, Bangladesh and worked with UC Davis Center for Regional Change.
Sumayyah Ahmed
Graduate Student Researcher & MS Candidate, Computer Science
Sumayyah Ahmed, a Master's student at the UC Davis Computer Science department. Her interests include web and mobile development, particularly in interactive interfaces and integrated technologies.
Prerna Dudani
Visual/User Experience Designer
Prerna Dudani is a maker and design activist who strongly believes that design solutions can empower people and communities to create meaningful change. She has collaborated with data visualization researchers in Computer Science at UC Davis and has also worked for an environmental graphics, signage, and wayfinding design firm in the San Francisco Bay Area.
Sarah Perrault
Assistant Professor, University Writing Program, Co-director
Sarah is Assistant Professor of Rhetoric and Writing in the University Writing Program. She holds an MFA in Nonfiction Creative Writing and a PhD in Rhetoric and Composition, and her research focuses on boundary-crossing written communication; her book, “Communicating Popular Science: From Deficit to Democracy,” was published in July 2013.
