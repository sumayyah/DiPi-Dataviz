EVOLUTION OF PARTICIPATORY PRACTICES:
5 disciplines, 50 years

DiPi History project is a joint effort between researchers, practitioners, and students to explore the evolution of participatory approaches overtime. A participatory approach is one in which everyone who has a stake in the intervention has a voice and influence through collaboration with practitioners and researchers. Under the collaborative effort, we investigated who and what have contributed to the evolution of participatory approaches overtime. Based on the research, we made a timeline that identifies key individuals, organizations, projects, and social events that influence the evolution of participatory approaches across five different fields. The fields include community design, youth development, international development, public health, and technology development.
We made this website in response to the growing needs among researchers and practitioners implanting participatory approaches. We hope that the timeline, groceries, and other resources help existing and future experts learn the evolution of participatory approaches. To explore the essence of collaborative works, we also documented and opened the process of collaborations as a resource for future collaborative projects. Please feel free to download and use these resources.
