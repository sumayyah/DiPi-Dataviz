UC DAVIS CENTER FOR DESIGN IN THE PUBLIC INTEREST (dipi)

Design Research
The UC Davis Center for Design In the Public Interest (dipi) offers design solutions to persistent problems affecting the public; researches collaborative, democratic design processes; and shares solutions and findings via Creative Commons licensing and scholarly publications. The newly launched Center was awarded seed funding through the UC Davis Office of Research Interdisciplinary Frontiers Program. Our multi-disciplinary team includes expertise in the humanities, arts, social sciences, engineering, and technology. It offers an experimental approach plus partnerships with faculty and students from diverse disciplines create unique opportunities to explore new methods and to work at the leading edge of a growing movement that connects human-centered design with social good.

TEAM

Susan Verba
Associate Professor, Department of Design, Cluster Leader/PI
Susan brings cross-disciplinary expertise in directing large collaborative project teams and developing innovative design methods, processes, and outcomes. Her academic research focuses on design for public benefit; she is particularly interested in addressing the needs of individuals outside the mainstream, including seniors, beginners, and persons with disabilities.
Yohei Kato
Graduate Student Researcher & MS Candidate, Community and Regional Development
Yohei Kato, a master's student at the UC Davis Community Development Graduate Group. His research interests include youth development and design that empowers community. He founded the competition in Dhaka, Bangladesh and facilitated local people to establish the Hajimari Art Center in Fukushima, Japan.
